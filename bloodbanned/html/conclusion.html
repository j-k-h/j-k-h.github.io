SHOULD I FORGO GAY SEX TO GIVE BLOOD?

Short answer? No. From what I heard at least, there seemed to be a pretty similar consensus from the three interviewees that for them personally, one shouldn’t forgo their sexuality to give blood. Further, it seemed like they all agreed that yes, the blood ban was discriminatory.


Not necessarily in the case of “this is blatant homophobia” but in the case of this could be improved. I think a lot of the responses from the people I talked to came from feeling attacked as a gay person, which is something that the current blood ban does - it’s confrontational.


So what’s my conclusion? I think that the blood ban, although sensical, needs to be re-written. I think that the negative implications that plague the red cross could be rewritten to an idea that’s more inclusive, that doesn’t automatically assume that men who sleep with other men are “dirty.”
