Should you forgo gay sex to give blood?

It's up to the individual honestly. I used to consider being celibate so that I could give blood, but as the years went on I noticed that it didn't really seem to matter - because I'm still going to be discredited either way, so you can't win. It's kind of hard to pin-point. There's just a lot of pushing and pulling coming from the whole situation; where I get why the ban is in place, but I still feel like it's outdated. Basically it bars the people who actually have a chance at giving blood just because of their lifestyle. Even without HIV there's still other diseases and stuff that can be passed through blood, so I feel like to just single out HIV is — HIV is bad, yes — but if they're going to do that they should be more strict in general.


"It bars the people who actually have a chance at giving blood just because of their lifestyle."

My answer is "I would not" and to them I would say "I can't make the decision for [you] but my personal advice would be to find another way to help."

I think that there's many different ways to help. Like, I'm a registered organ donor. I don't think you should have to give up your sexual life to do something like donating blood. My answer is "I would not" and to them I would say "I can't make the decision for them but my personal advice would be to find another way to help."

[If a friend were to ask this] I would support them of course, if they feel so strongly to where 'this is very important to me, I need to give blood,' I support that. I hate that they would have to do that, because it seems very idiotic to not have a proper testing system within donating blood. The blood is being tested, why aren't you just applying that to everyone else. Why aren't you applying that to those who are "more at risk" of getting blood-transmitted diseases. It's just — I would support them. I want to donate blood, unfortunately how I am and how my life is right now, it just wouldn't be realistic for me. It wouldn't make sense for me to be 100% abstinent. Hopefully [the ban] will change soon.
