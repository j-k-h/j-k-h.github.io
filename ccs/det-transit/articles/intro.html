An Introduction

Welcome to the 2020 Detroit Transit Report: a birds-eye evaluation of public transportation’s past, present, and future in Detroit. This e-publication will outline what made Detroit’s transit what it is today, current barriers and achievements, and a pragmatic set of solutions. Further, this publication will outline the two authors’ concentrations within the topic moving forward.


This report is a product of a junior studio at the College for Creative Studies which evaluates sustainability and Detroit’s longevity. By utilizing various methods of research, we’ve constructed what we feel is a comprehensive survey of Detroit’s transit situation. It is not incredibly in-depth (as each point of research could be an entire publication in of itself), but instead key points that tell an overarching story.


Using IDEO’s research methodology, we conducted the following analysis:


Phase one: understanding the current system

  • Flow Analysis: what steps and stages comprise Detroit’s public transportation?
  • Error Analysis: what’s wrong with transportation in Detroit?
  • Historical Analysis: what does the history of local transportation look like?
  • Secondary Research: what research has already been done about transportation in Detroit?


Phase two: experiencing the current system

  • Interviews: what personal experiences have Detroit residents had with public transit?
  • Fly on the Wall: how do people behave/what are people’s habits when using public transportation?


Phase three: explore future solutions

  • Long-Range Forecasts: the future of transportation and the people involved
  • Anthropometric Analysis: human population data to check the suitability of products/systems
