What the People Need

With Detroit having a 36% poverty rate and 66% vehicle ownership, our city needs to begin designing for the people farther than midtown who need public transportation as a means of commuting to work, school, and home.

Our city needs to begin designing for the people farther than midtown who need public transportation as a means of commuting to work, school, and home.

For the transit that does reach that far, it needs to be more reliable and kept up. Detroiters need more reliable sidewalks through all seasons, conditions, and neighborhoods. They need more sustainable, inclusive, and low cost transportation. And they need systems that are interconnected through all parts of the city for convenience and efficiency.
