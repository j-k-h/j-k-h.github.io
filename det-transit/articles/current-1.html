Today

Detroit’s current-day public transit system is, of course, an outcome of its past. As Detroit’s population has declined and its economy has suffered since its heyday in the mid 20th century, its services like public transit have dropped in priority and consequently quality. The city’s poverty rate is far above the national average at 36% in 2018, and 34% of Detroiters don’t own a car according to a study done by the University of Michigan. Detroit’s current population is under half what it was 50 years ago.
